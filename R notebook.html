How many movies were released from 1/01/2015 to 31/12/2021 ?

summarise(bollywood_df, Total = n())

There were 684 movies released in theater and on OTT platforms from 1-01-2015 to 31-12-2021.

Which year produced lowest number of films and highest number films ? What is the average production of the movies per year ?

movies_produced_yearly <- bollywood_df %>% group_by(Released_Year) %>%
                                        summarise(Total_movies = n())
movies_produced_yearly
round(mean(movies_produced_yearly$Total_movies))
## [1] 98

Lowest production year was 2020 with a total of 66 movies, and Highest production year was 2018 and 2019, both years producing a total of 127 movies with an average production of movies per year is 98.

Does the production of movies increase or decrease year by year ? Is there any trend and can we find out why ?

graph_1 <- movies_produced_yearly %>%
  ggplot(aes(x=Released_Year, y=Total_movies)) + 
  geom_col(fill = "#1E90FF") +
  labs(x="Years", y="Total movies", title = "Bollywood Movies Released", subtitle = "From 1/01/2015 to 31/12/2021") +
  geom_text(aes(label = Total_movies), vjust = 3 ,  col = "White") +
  theme(axis.text.y=element_blank(), axis.ticks.y=element_blank()) +
  geom_line(color="green",size=1.5) + 
  geom_point()

graph_1

As we can see from the graph, the production of the movies increased year by year till 2019 as 2018 and 2019 both produced the same amount of movies and then production of the movies suddenly dropped in 2020. The reason was due to pandemic and strict lockdown was followed in India due to this all production was closed and many movies were released directly on OTT platforms, which is why some movies never revealed the budget and earnings. But there is a rise in 2021.

To find this answer I have conducted a pearson correlation test between budget and box office

ref <- cor.test(top_100_box_office$Budget_in_crore.INR., top_100_box_office$Worldwide_box_office_gross_in_crore.INR.)
correlation_1 <- round(ref$estimate, digits = 4)

Final Conclusion

Movies with higher budgets might perform well at the box office, but in terms of ratings it is not necessary to have a higher budget to win the hearts of the audience. Movies with higher budgets perform well at the box office because of their various promotion techniques but for a movie to be called successful, it should perform well at the box office and also in terms of user ratings.
